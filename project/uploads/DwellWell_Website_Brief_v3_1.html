This document specifies the DwellWell website end to end — its purpose, audiences, structure, page content, forms, and the brand system applied to the web. It is written so the site can be built in Claude Design with no prior context on the business.

SECTION 01

The Business

Everything on the site must reflect one positioning: DwellWell is the operator of record, not a platform, marketplace, or directory. (Brand Guidelines v2.5 §01.)

Positioning

DwellWell Property Care LLC is a US-based managed property maintenance and preservation operator — the single point of coordination between the demand side (property managers, landlords, lenders, real-estate investors) and vetted, independent US-based contractors. DwellWell receives work orders, manages the full job lifecycle from intake to closeout, dispatches contractors, and owns the client relationship throughout. When something goes wrong, the client deals with DwellWell — not the contractor.

What DwellWell is notNot a gig platform, a marketplace, a directory, or a startup. The site never adopts consumer-app, gig-economy, or home-services-franchise cues. The word "platform" is prohibited in all copy.

The anchor sentence — all site copy derives from this

Locked · D-02

"DwellWell coordinates property maintenance from intake to closeout — one point of contact, vetted contractors, every job documented."

Every headline, sub-line, and section lead on the site is a restatement or fragment of this sentence. If a piece of copy cannot be traced back to it, rewrite the copy.

The three brand words

Every page is tested against three words before it ships.

SECTION 02

Goals & Audiences

The site has a primary job, a secondary job, and three audiences — each scanning for different evidence.

What the website must do

  1. Generate leads from property managers and preservation clients — primarily through a Contact flow.
  2. Capture service requests and quote enquiries — through a dedicated request form.

A secondary goal is to recruit contractors to the network via a simple application form. Keep this path visible but understated — never at parity with the primary client audience.

The three audiences

The voice is identical across audiences (Locked · D-03). The register — what each page emphasises and assumes — shifts to each audience's context, fear, and decision criteria. (Brand Guidelines v2.5 §02.)

CodeAudienceThe site leads with
PM (primary)Property management professionals — owners, operations directors, portfolio managers. B2B buyers: busy, analytical, sceptical. They scan headings and the service list; they read only if the scan earns it.The managed-operator distinction. One contact. The documentation standard. Accountability owned by DwellWell. Reduce language — "fewer vendors," "no chasing required."
PR (secondary)Preservation clients — lenders, banks, investors managing vacant or distressed assets.Documentation completeness. Compliance awareness. Preservation scope specifically. Response speed for time-sensitive securing tasks.
CO (tertiary)Contractors — independent tradespeople in or applying to the network.Clear scope on every job. Consistent, managed dispatch. Professional coordination. "Apply to join" signals selectivity.

The site speaks naturally to PM and PR without separate sections for each. The CO path is a single page plus a footer link.

Personality the site must project

DwellWell speaks like a senior partner at a well-regarded professional firm — formal enough to command respect, human enough to treat clients as people. Calm, authoritative, competent. The test: "DwellWell is the most competent person in the room — and doesn't need to say so." If a page sounds like it is trying to impress, it is wrong; if it sounds like it is describing how things work, it is right.

SECTION 08

Trust & Compliance

With no testimonials at launch, trust is communicated through process and precise, defensible claims. Every claim below sits inside the messaging architecture (Brand Guidelines v2.5 §02).

8.1 — Coverage statement (locked wording)

Use consistently — homepage, service overviews, Coverage page"Multi-state coverage across the US and expanding. Service availability depends on location and contractor coverage — contact us to confirm your area."

Never write "nationwide coverage" — coverage is case-by-case. "Across the US" is the approved directional phrasing.

8.2 — Warranty (gated — do not publish terms)

Open · D-04 (Decision 2-B)Warranty language is not approved for public copy until US counsel reviews the wording. Do not publish warranty terms, timeframes, conditions, or implied guarantees anywhere on the site. Any warranty figure carried in earlier briefs (e.g. a 30-day workmanship period) is internal planning language only — not approved copy — and is superseded by this gate until legal sign-off.

Practical effect on the build: no warranty figures on the homepage, service pages, or Service Terms; the Warranty / Rework page shell is built but unpublished and unlinked until counsel approves.

8.3 — Contractor standards (what to say publicly)

Approved wording
"We work with experienced, professional field vendors who meet our documentation and service standards. Every job is tracked, verified, and closed out with photos and written confirmation."

Do not claim contractor licensing or insurance status on the site. "Vetted contractors" is approved at concept level — do not elaborate on vetting specifics, and do not write "licensed/insured/background-checked," until operations confirms.

8.4 — Responsiveness (scoped) & process as trust

Scoped response claim — the only publishable version"Emergency and urgent requests receive a same-business-day acknowledgement."

No standard-request response window is publishable until operations confirms one — a blanket "we respond to all enquiries the same business day" must not appear. Replace it wherever it surfaced in the old brief (Contact page, thank-you page).

  • How it works on the homepage (5–7 steps) — the primary trust element.
  • Documentation stated generally — every job is documented and closed out with photos and written confirmation. Do not specify photo counts, report formats, or system names.
  • Accountability — DwellWell owns the resolution process; clients do not deal directly with contractors.
